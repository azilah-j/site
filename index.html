lebok /ləˈbɒk/ — pronounced luh-BOCK

Lebok is a word from the Melanau language.
It means "house" or "home".

The Melanau language is rich with a variation of dialects.
It is primarily due to the geography of Melanau communities living along Sarawak's vast coastline and river systems.
The dialects are often so different that they are not mutually intelligible. Commonly neighboring dialects can understand each other, but not for those further apart.

This variation — lebok /ləˈbɒk/ — reflects the Mukah dialect,
a major Melanau dialect spoken in the Sarawak central coastal region.

Another variation is lebuk, pronounced luh-BUUK.
More variations are kubou, and lamin,
with lamin also referring to the ancestral Melanau tall house.
